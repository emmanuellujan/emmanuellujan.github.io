5G Narrowband IoT Modeling

One of the IoT challenges is providing communication support to an increasing number of sensors. In recent years, a narrowband radio technology has emerged to address this situation: Narrowband Internet of Things (NB-IoT), which is an integral part of 5G. Despite of the efforts, massive connectivity become particularly demanding in extreme coverage scenarios such as underground or deep inside buildings sites. Here we propose a novel strategy for this coverage contexts focused on optimizing NB-IoT shared channels through the adaptation of the link parameters: modulation and coding scheme, as well as the number of repetitions. These parameters are defined at the base station (BS) with each block transmitted, until reaching a target block error rate (BLER t ) or range (i.e. QoS). A wrong selection of these magnitudes leads to radio resource waste, decreasing the number of concurrent connections. Specifically, our strategy is based on a look-up table (LUT) scheme, used for rapidly delivering the optimal link parameters given a desire QoS, in terms of resource usage and block losses. Strategy assessment is performed over an open source NB-IoT uplink simulator, designed for this specific purpose. A set of alternative strategies is compared, transmitting blocks of 256 bits under an AWGN model over the NPUSCH. Results show that few QoS levels are actually available, favoring robustness against measurement uncertainties. Our strategy minimizes resource usage in all scenarios of acknowledged mode, and remarkably reduced losses in the unacknowledged mode, presenting also substantial gains in performance. We expect to influence future BS software design and implementation, favoring connection support under extreme environments.
